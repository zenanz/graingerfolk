Percy Grainger made a number of recordings and notations of the song I’m 17 come Sunday between September 1905 and August 1906. Grainger heard nine different singers perform this song, in various places in northern England including Redbourne, Brigg, Barrow-on-Humber, Tickhill, and Bawtry in Yorkshire. Grainger phonographed four of these performances.

I’m 17 come Sunday

I’m 17 Come Sunday, sung by Mr Fred Atkinson at the home of Mr W. Goodhand (farmer), Redbourne, Somerset, UK, on 3 September 1905. Phonographed and noted by Percy Grainger on Wax Cylinder Number 48; Song no 125 in Grainger’s English Folk Song Collection. Grainger noted that the song was ‘very rhythmic and accented, not too fast’.
